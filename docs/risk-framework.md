# BCTrust Risk Framework

BCTrust evaluates Web3 assets using several risk categories.

## 1. Liquidity Risk

Reviews whether a token has enough liquidity to support healthy trading.

Signals may include:

- Low liquidity
- Large price impact
- Unlocked liquidity
- Sudden liquidity removal

## 2. Holder Concentration Risk

Reviews whether a small number of wallets control a large percentage of token supply.

Signals may include:

- Top holders controlling high supply percentage
- Developer wallet concentration
- Exchange wallet concentration
- Suspicious wallet clustering

## 3. Contract Risk

Reviews whether the token contract has potential risk indicators.

Signals may include:

- Unverified contract
- Unknown owner permissions
- Transfer restrictions
- Minting permissions
- Blacklist or whitelist controls

## 4. Ownership Risk

Reviews whether contract ownership is renounced, controlled, or unclear.

Signals may include:

- Active owner wallet
- Admin privileges
- Upgradeable contracts
- Unknown permission structure

## 5. Market Activity Risk

Reviews whether trading activity appears healthy or suspicious.

Signals may include:

- Very low volume
- Sudden volume spikes
- Extreme price volatility
- Wash-trading concerns

## 6. Tokenomics Risk

Reviews whether the token supply and distribution model appears sustainable.

Signals may include:

- Poor supply distribution
- Unclear vesting
- High insider allocation
- Weak utility
- Excessive emissions

## 7. Rug-Pull Risk

Reviews combined red flags that may suggest a token has elevated rug-pull risk.

Signals may include:

- Unlocked liquidity
- Anonymous team
- Unverified contract
- High holder concentration
- Suspicious owner controls
- Poor market depth
